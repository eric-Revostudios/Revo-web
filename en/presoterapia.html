What happens to your body

Compression that moves what you don’t drain on your own.

Six air chambers inflate in sequence, bottom to top, pushing blood and lymph toward the heart. That improves venous return, activates the lymphatic system and reduces fluid build-up: lighter legs, less inflammation and better muscle recovery.
